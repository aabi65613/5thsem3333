Q4(b). Compare Jig and Fixture (5)

Answer: Jig = holds + guides tool (like drill guide). Fixture = only holds job (no guide).

  • Jig: For drilling/milling, has bush, costlier, skilled design.
  • Fixture: For turning, just clamps, cheaper, unskilled.

Q5. Explain 3-2-1 principle of location with neat sketch (5)

Answer: To stop a block from moving 6 ways (3 slides + 3 turns), use pins:

  • 3 pins bottom: Stops up-down, left-right, front-back.
  • 2 pins one side: Stops rolling.
  • 1 pin opposite: Stops tilting.

Q8(b). Write meaning of lathe program: N02 G90 I500 F0.5; N03 G50 S2000; N04 G96 S200 M03; N05 G00 X102 Z2; N20 Z0.5; N30 G01 X92 F0.3; N40 X82; N50 X72; N60 X62; N70 X52; N80 X42; N90 X32; N100 X22; N110 X12; N120 G00 X102; N130 G01 Z-20 F0.2; N140 G00 X0; N150 M05; N160 M30 (6)

Answer (Step-by-Step):

  • N02: Absolute mode, inch, feed 0.5
  • N03: Max spindle 2000 RPM
  • N04: Constant speed 200, spindle ON
  • N05-N120: Rapid to start, then straight cuts stepping X by 10 (turning diameters)
  • N130: Feed along Z -20mm (length cut)
  • N150-160: Stop, end program

Q9. Briefly discuss about FMS layout configurations. What are adv/disadv of FMS? (4)

Answer: FMS = Flexible Manufacturing System (many machines auto-change for different parts).

  • Layouts: Linear (straight line), Loop (circle), Open (free move).
  • Adv: Fast switch parts, less worker, high variety.
  • Disadv: Costly setup, complex control.
